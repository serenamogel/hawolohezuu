Early Life:

Jake Anderson, born as Jacob Van Martin Anderson on September 16, 1980, in Anacortes, Washington, is an American fishing captain and TV personality. He grew up in a fishing town, following his father Keith Arthur Anderson, who was a U.S. Army veteran and a fourth-generation fisherman. He also had a sister, Chelsea Dawn Anderson, who died unexpectedly in 2009.

As a child, Jake was passionate about skateboarding and dreamed of becoming a professional skater. However, a severe ankle injury shattered his hopes and led him to alcohol and drug addiction. He spent two years living on the streets, struggling with his demons. He finally realized that he had to give up skateboarding and find a new purpose in life. He decided to pursue a career in fishing, following his father’s footsteps and his family’s tradition. 

Professional Career:

Jake Anderson is an American fishing captain and co-owner of the F/V Saga, a crab fishing vessel that operates in the Bering Sea. He started his career as a salmon fisherman in Bristol Bay, Alaska, but in 2007, he got a chance to join the crew of the F/V Northwestern, one of the most successful boats in the Discovery Channel documentary series Deadliest Catch. Under the mentorship of Captain Sig Hansen, Jake learned the ropes of the dangerous and lucrative crab fishing industry, and rose from a greenhorn to a deck boss in 2012. That same year, he obtained his USCG Mate 1600-ton license and Master 100-ton Captain’s license, and in 2015, he became the captain and co-owner of the F/V Saga. Since then, he has been leading his own crew on the Saga, except for a brief period in 2018 when he switched to the F/V Kiska Sea. Jake is also a professional skateboarder, a bestselling author, and a father of three.

Deadliest Catch:

Deadliest Catch is a thrilling and captivating reality TV show that takes viewers on a journey into the world of crab fishing in Alaska. Since 2005, the show has been airing on the Discovery Channel and reaching audiences across the globe. The show follows the adventures and struggles of various captains and crews who brave the icy and stormy conditions of the Bering Sea in search of the lucrative king crab and snow crab. One of the most prominent and inspiring figures on the show is Jake Anderson, the captain and co-owner of the Saga.

Jake started his career as a greenhorn on the Northwestern, where he learned the ropes from the legendary Sig Hansen. After proving his skills and resilience, Jake became the deck boss of the Northwestern and earned his captain’s licenses such as USCG Mate 1600-ton license and Master 100-ton Captain’s license.

In 2015, Jake took a leap of faith and bought the Saga from its former captain Elliot Neese. Since then, Jake has been leading his crew with courage and determination, overcoming personal and professional challenges along the way. Jake has appeared in 216 episodes of Deadliest Catch as the captain of the Saga from 2007 to 2022. He has also starred in several spin-offs and specials of the show, such as Deadliest Catch: The Bait, where he shares his insights and opinions on the fishing season, Deadliest Catch: Inside the Catch, where he reveals behind-the-scenes secrets and stories, Deadliest Catch: Trial of a Generation, where he faces a historic pandemic and a rival Russian fleet, and Deadliest Catch: The Viking Returns, where he teams up with his mentor Sig Hansen to catch more crab.

Family Tragedies:

Jake Anderson has faced many personal tragedies in his life despite his success as a fisherman. In 2009, he received the devastating news that his sister, Chelsea Dawn Anderson, had died unexpectedly from complications due to pneumonia. She was 37 and had a history of juvenile rheumatoid arthritis and. Jake paid tribute to her on the 11th anniversary of her passing and shared his struggles with her loss.
