Lena Meyer-Landrut is a German singer-songwriter. She rose to fame after winning the Eurovision Song Contest 2010 in Oslo, with her song Satellite. Both “Satellite” and her debut album, My Cassette Player (2010) appeared at number one in Germany and became platinum sellers.

Career

Lena Meyer-Landrut released her successful albums, including Good News (2011), Stardust (2012), Crystal Sky (2015), and Only Love, L (2019), all of which appeared in the top two of the German Albums Chart.

She is the recipient of many accolades, including the 1LIVE Krone Award, Echo, MTV Europe Music Awards, and Nickelodeon Kids Choice Awards. She grew up as an only child and started taking dancing lessons at five. In June 2010, she graduated from IGS Roderbruch Hannover, earning her Abitur diploma.

With her three entries from the German national final, Our Star for Oslo. She set an all-time chart record by debuting with three songs in the top five of the German Singles Chart. She represented Germany for the second successive time in the Eurovision Song Contest 2011.

In addition, she gave voice to the characters in animated films, including A Turtle’s Tale (2010), Tarzan (2013), Trolls (2016), and Wonder Park (2019). Lena Meyer-Landrut has been a brand ambassador for L’Oréal and has become one of the best-selling female German artists to rise in the early 2010s.

She has millions of followers on Instagram. However, she usually refuses to answer questions about her private life. This has drawn praise and criticism, including accusations of being arrogant and disrespectful.
