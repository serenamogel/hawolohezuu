Sharpshooter Tom Knapp's Works and Achievements

Sharpshooter Tom has achieved the best 3 world records of his life for hand-thrown clay targets. He initially sets out his first World record by shooting within less than 2 seconds with his M1 Super 90 in 1993. Moreover, he threw eight clay targets in the air with his pump shotgun in one hand within 1.87 seconds in 2000. With 12-ga M2 Field semi-auto shotgun, he launched ten clay targets into the air with one hand within 2.2 seconds in 2004.

One of Knapp's Benelli shotgun shots videotape gained the most views on YouTube in 2007, where he is firing from his pump-action weapon from the behind of his back and from over of his head, each time hitting his airborne targets. In one scene, he hurls his shotgun into the air, flings a clay target skyward behind him and catches his gun, and fires by leaving an orange puff of dust.

Is Shooting Star Tom Knapp Still Alive or Dead?

The Minnesota-based sharpshooter, Knapp took his last breath at the age of 62 on Friday, 26th April 2013, in Rochester Minnesota, the United States at St. Mary’s Hospital at the Mayo Clinic in Rochester. Three years before his demise, he was suffering from many medical problems and the cause of his death was pulmonary fibrosis. 

There was no funeral service because Tom did not wish to have it. Later, all of his family and friends arrived for the burial service at 1 p.m. on 11th May 2013 at the Del Tone/Luth Gun Club in St. Cloud. Although Tom did not want to have a funeral, the Knapp family decided to gather for memorial service at the Pulmonary Fibrosis Foundation.
